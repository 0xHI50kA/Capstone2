Health News: Understanding Runny Nose

What is a Runny Nose?

A runny nose, medically referred to as rhinorrhea, occurs when there is an excessive discharge of fluid from the nasal passages. This common condition can be triggered by various factors including colds, allergies, environmental irritants, or infections. People of all ages may experience a runny nose, and while often harmless, it can occasionally indicate underlying health concerns.

Possible Causes

Understanding the root cause of a runny nose can aid in its management. Below are common triggers:

  • Common Cold
  • Allergies
  • Sinus Infection
  • Weather Changes

Simple Diagnosis

Identifying accompanying symptoms can help determine the cause of a runny nose:

  • Allergies: Sneezing, itchy eyes, and nasal congestion often suggest an allergic reaction.
  • Colds: A sore throat, fatigue, and fever typically point to a viral infection.

For mild cases, rest, hydration, and over-the-counter medications may provide relief. However, persistent or severe symptoms should prompt a consultation with a healthcare provider to rule out serious conditions.
